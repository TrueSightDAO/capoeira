Community Life at Tribo Bahia Mirim

Capoeira is more than the game in the roda — it is the community that surrounds it. Below are moments from life at Tribo Bahia Mirim in Itacaré: ceremonies that mark a student's path, feast days that carry centuries of Afro-Brazilian tradition.

Batizado — The Capoeira Baptism

The batizado is the milestone ceremony where capoeira students receive their first corda (rope marking their level) and a capoeira nickname. It is the day a student is born into the capoeira community.

São Miguel's Feast Day — Caruru for the Kids

On 29 September, the community gathers and caruru — a traditional Bahian dish of okra, dendê palm oil, dried shrimp, and ground nuts — is served to the children. The dish carries the tradition of feeding seven children who answer "sim, eu como" ("yes, I will eat") to the syncretic saints Cosme and Damião, an offering of generosity that bridges Catholic and Candomblé practice.

Support keeps the program running — every dollar (minus fees) goes to Bico Duro and the kids.
